Fayrouz's Life

Nouhad Wadie Haddad, known as Fairuz, is a Lebanese singer. She is widely considered an iconic vocalist and one of the most celebrated singers in the history of the Arab world. She is popularly known as "The Bird of the East", "The Cedar of Lebanon", "The Moon's Neighbor", and "The Voice of Lebanon", among others.

Name's background

The singer Fairouz was called Fayrouz because Halim Al-Rumi, the head of Lebanese Radio, suggested she adopt the stage name. Al-Rumi gave her the name "Fairuz," which means "turquoise" or "precious stone" in Arabic, because her voice reminded him of the precious stone. She was born Nuhad al-Haddad and initially thought the suggestion was a joke, but she later took his advice.
